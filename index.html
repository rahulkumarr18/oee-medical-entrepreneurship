Physicians see the problems first

Doctors spot unmet needs before anyone else. Very few are ever shown how one of those needs becomes a device, a diagnostic, or a company. That gap is what this elective closes.

The core curriculum covers the science and the medicine. This covers what comes after the idea: founders and scientific advisors, biotech and medical devices, digital health and diagnostics, tech transfer, incubators, and how ventures get funded. It runs as a series of evening dinners with people who have built and backed real companies, and it is meant for the curious, not only for people already set on starting something.

The point

The point is the network

Every session ends the same way: dinner, and a room full of people worth knowing. Students leave with real contacts across the New England innovation ecosystem, the kind you can call during residency and long after.

Physician-founders

Doctors who have started biotech, device, and digital-health companies, and can tell you what the path actually looks like.

Investors

Venture partners and angels who fund early healthcare companies and decide what gets built.

Biotech & medtech leaders

Operators from drug discovery, diagnostics, and medical devices across the New England corridor.

Incubators & accelerators

The people who run tech transfer, incubators, and accelerators where clinical ideas get their start.

Nucleate

An introduction to the national bio-entrepreneurship community for students who want to go deeper.

Peer schools

Meet-ups with innovation groups at Harvard, Tufts, and BU medical schools, UMass Lowell engineering, and Harvard Business School healthcare clubs.

Network capital: you finish with a map of the regional ecosystem and the introductions to go with it.

Fall 2026

Six evenings

Every other Wednesday, 6:00 to 7:30 PM, in the Albert Sherman Center on the Worcester campus. Thirty minutes of talk, thirty of questions, thirty over dinner. The last session moves to Boston/Cambridge for an off-site networking night.

    Kickoff and a working primer on the business model canvas.

    Nathaniel Hafer, PhD
  2. Sep 30

    Drug discovery and the pharmaceutical path

    How a therapeutic actually gets made, and how it differs from biotech.

    Alan Jacobson, PhD · co-developer of Ataluren
  3. Oct 14

    Physician as founder

    Margaret Koziel, MD
  4. Oct 28

    Boston networking night

    An off-site evening in Boston/Cambridge, paired with a candid look at startups that failed and why.

    Student leaders and invited founders
