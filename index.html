In plain terms: what is this?

A community for people curious about how medical ideas become real products and companies: a device, a diagnostic, an app, or a new treatment. It started as a UMass Chan elective and is open to students, physicians, professors, and founders across Massachusetts. Come to a dinner to learn, bring an early idea for feedback, or sign up to give a talk. No business background needed.

Built for more than one campus

Open to the Massachusetts medical innovation community.

The UMass Chan OEE track gives medical students a structured path and certificate. The incubator around it is broader: students from nearby schools, engineers, residents, fellows, physicians, faculty, entrepreneurs, and speakers can join events, mentor teams, or present their work.

That wider mix is the point. A student with an idea needs more than a lecture. They need a clinician who has seen the problem, an engineer who can prototype, a founder who knows the first market, a professor who can sharpen the science, and someone in Boston who knows where the next conversation should happen.
