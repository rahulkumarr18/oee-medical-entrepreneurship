Pitch and showcase

Your idea does not need to be polished. It needs a room.

Bring an observation, rough concept, prototype, research direction, or early venture. We turn it into a conversation with people who can sharpen it: clinicians, engineers, founders, and investors from across Massachusetts and the Boston corridor.

How it works

From a rough idea to your first real pitch, in front of people who can help.

01 · Notice

Start with a problem

A delay, a device that fails the user, a workflow nobody likes, a diagnostic gap, a policy barrier, or a clinical question that keeps coming back.

02 · Shape

Make the idea legible

Use a simple one-page worksheet to think through who has the problem, why your idea helps, what proof you have, who would use or pay for it first, and what to learn next.

03 · Present

Pitch to a friendly room

Five minutes in front of students, clinicians, faculty, founders, investors, and engineers, drawn from across Massachusetts and the Boston medical corridor. The point is useful feedback, not theater.

What belongs on stage

Projects can be very early. A good pitch might be a clinical pain point, a device concept, an AI workflow, a diagnostic idea, a health policy case, a private-practice operations problem, or a research discovery with translational potential.

The incubator is not looking for perfect companies. It is looking for ideas that deserve a better first conversation.
