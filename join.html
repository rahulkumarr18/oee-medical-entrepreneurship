Fall 2026

Join the first cohort

Open to T.H. Chan School of Medicine students. The first cohort is capped at 25, on purpose, so the evenings stay a conversation.

What you are signing up for

Six evenings across the fall, every other Wednesday from 6:00 to 7:30 PM, mostly on the UMass Chan campus in Worcester, with a final networking night in Boston or Cambridge. Dinner is provided.

To earn the certificate of completion in Medical Entrepreneurship and Innovation, come to at least five of the six sessions, keep a short reflection after each, and build one one-page business model canvas. That is the whole ask.

No experience needed. You do not need a background in business, a startup, or even an idea.

Questions

Good to know

Do I need a business background or an idea?
No. The elective is built for curious students first. You do not need a startup, a plan, or any business coursework. If you want to bring an early idea, there is room for that too, but it is optional.
Does it cost anything?
No. It is free, and dinner is provided at each session.
How much time is it?
Six evenings across the fall, 6:00 to 7:30 PM every other Wednesday, plus a short written reflection after each session and one one-page business model canvas over the term.
Is it graded?
No. It is pass or complete, not graded. Attend at least five of the six sessions, keep your reflections, and turn in one business model canvas to earn a certificate of completion.
Who can join, and how many spots are there?
It is open to T.H. Chan School of Medicine students. The Fall 2026 cohort is capped at 25, on purpose, so the evenings stay a conversation.
Do I have to present a project?
No. Presenting is optional. Many students will just come, listen, ask questions, and meet people. See the showcase if you do want to bring an idea.
What do I actually get out of it?
A clearer picture of how clinical ideas become companies, and real contacts across the region: founders, investors, and operators you can email later. Plus the certificate.
