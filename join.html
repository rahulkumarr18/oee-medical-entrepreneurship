For the UMass Chan OEE track

Six evenings across Fall 2026, with meetings in Worcester and Boston/Cambridge. Dinner is provided.

To earn the certificate, attend at least five sessions, complete short reflections, and submit one business model canvas. The OEE track is capped so the sessions stay conversational.

No business background is required. You can arrive with a startup idea, a clinical observation, or just curiosity.

Questions

Good to know

Do I need a startup idea?
No. Many people will come to learn and meet people. If you do have an idea, the incubator gives you a place to test it early.
Is it only for UMass students?
No. The OEE credit track is for UMass Chan students, but the incubator is built to include students, trainees, faculty, founders, and speakers across Massachusetts.
Where will events happen?
The home base is Worcester, with major networking and showcase events planned for Boston and Cambridge so the broader region can participate.
Can professors or founders speak?
Yes. We are actively looking for people who can speak honestly about biotech, medtech, digital health, AI, private practice, consulting, health law, reimbursement, policy, and venture building.
Can a company support an event?
Yes, with clear guardrails. Sponsorship supports logistics such as dinners and hosting. It does not buy promotion, student data, or influence over student projects.
