Guests

Speakers, by theme

The people students hear from, grouped by the kind of path they took. This is the planned roster; each guest is confirmed closer to their session.

Bench to biotech how a discovery becomes a therapeutic

Allan Jacobson, PhD
Distinguished professor and chair emeritus of microbiology at UMass Chan, and co-founder of PTC Therapeutics; his work on nonsense-mediated mRNA decay helped inform the development of the drug ataluren. A bench-to-biotech case study.
Margaret Koziel, MD
Chief Medical Officer of Boston Pharmaceuticals; an infectious-disease physician-scientist who moved from academic medicine, including UMass Chan and Harvard, into drug-development leadership.

Diagnostics and devices getting tools to the point of care

Nathaniel Hafer, PhD
Associate professor at UMass Chan who develops and evaluates point-of-care diagnostics; co-leads the NIH-funded CAPCaT center and helped coordinate studies for the RADx Tech rapid-testing program. Opens the term on the innovation landscape.

Digital health and AI software, data, and clinical decisions

Aditya Jain, MD
Harvard-trained physician and AI researcher at the Broad Institute who co-founded Prescience, a Y Combinator-backed startup using machine learning to lower the cost of employer healthcare.
Amar Vadhera
Medical student at Sidney Kimmel Medical College and a prolific orthopedic sports-medicine researcher, with recent work on artificial intelligence in orthopedic care.

Medicine at the edge space, imaging, and new frontiers

Joshua Ong, MD
Ophthalmologist and NASA-funded space-medicine researcher studying spaceflight-associated eye disease (SANS) and AI imaging; Forbes 30 Under 30, now in cornea and refractive surgery fellowship training at Harvard and Mass Eye and Ear.
Ethan Waisberg, MB BCh BAO
Physician-researcher at the University of Cambridge whose AI vision-testing system now flies on the International Space Station; co-founded AngioGenius to adapt that imaging into at-home eye screening.

Founders and investors building and funding early companies

Invited digital-health founder
Building and financing an early digital-health or diagnostics company, with a candid account of what the first few years actually take.
Invited venture investor
Funding early healthcare companies and deciding, in practice, what gets built. Joins the final session to give feedback on student ideas.
