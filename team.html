Speaker lanes

What we want students and project teams to hear.

These are the categories we are building around. Named guests are confirmed separately as the calendar firms up.

Founder path

Physicians who built companies

What changed when a clinical problem became a venture, and what they would do differently the second time.

Academic translation

Professors moving science outward

How a discovery becomes intellectual property, a prototype, a company, a trial, or a partnership.

Medtech

Device builders and operators

Clinical need finding, design constraints, prototyping, regulatory path, and first customers.

Digital health and AI

Software in real workflows

What actually has to happen before AI or digital health tools are trusted in care delivery.

Capital

Investors and angels

How early companies are evaluated, why some ideas get funded, and what makes a pitch credible.

Policy and law

Regulation, reimbursement, health law

The legal, payment, advocacy, and implementation questions that decide whether an idea reaches patients.

What makes a strong talk

The best talks are practical and specific. Students do not need a polished success story as much as they need the real sequence: what problem you noticed, who helped, what blocked the path, how money or policy shaped the work, and what you wish you had known earlier.

We also welcome case discussions, pitch feedback, office hours, Boston meetups, and project mentoring. A professor can teach the science. A founder can explain the first customer. A lawyer can make AI regulation legible. An investor can show why a pitch lands or does not.
