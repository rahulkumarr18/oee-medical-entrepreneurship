Speakers

The people you hear from.

Founders, physicians, and researchers who come to tell students how a medical idea actually becomes a product, a company, or a career. This roster grows as the Fall 2026 calendar firms up.

Space medicine & AI

Joshua Ong, MD

Ophthalmologist and NASA-funded space-medicine researcher studying spaceflight-associated eye disease and AI imaging; Forbes 30 Under 30, now in cornea and refractive surgery fellowship training at Harvard and Mass Eye and Ear.

Space medicine & AI

Ethan Waisberg, MB BCh BAO

Physician-researcher at the University of Cambridge whose AI vision-testing system now flies on the International Space Station; co-founded AngioGenius to turn that imaging into at-home eye screening.

Digital health & AI

Aditya Jain, MD

Harvard-trained physician and Broad Institute AI researcher who co-founded Prescience, a Y Combinator-backed startup using machine learning to lower the cost of employer healthcare.

Orthopedics & AI

Amar Vadhera, MD

Orthopedic sports-medicine researcher with a large body of published work, including recent work on artificial intelligence in orthopedic care.

Student founder

Elise Pham

Founder and CEO of Ultimate Ivy League Guide, who speaks to what it takes to build and run a company as a student, from first idea to real customers.

This is the growing roster; guests are confirmed closer to each session. You will also hear from the session leads on the Events page, including Nathaniel Hafer, PhD, Allan Jacobson, PhD, and Margaret Koziel, MD.

Speaker themes

What we want you to hear, whether you are just learning or already building.

The kinds of talks we build the calendar around.

Founder path

Physicians who built companies

What changed when a clinical problem became a venture, and what they would do differently the second time.

From lab to market

Professors turning research into products

How a discovery in a lab becomes a patent, a prototype, a company, a trial, or a partnership.

Digital health & AI

Health software and AI that doctors actually use

What it really takes before a new app or AI tool is trusted in care delivery.

Capital

Investors and angels

How early companies are evaluated, why some ideas get funded, and what makes a pitch credible.

Policy & law

Regulation, reimbursement, health law

The legal, payment, and policy questions that decide whether an idea reaches patients.

Private practice

Building outside a big institution

Careers and companies that grow through private practice and independent operators.
