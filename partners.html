How support works

Support pays for running the events and getting students in the door; it does not buy influence over what we teach or who we invite. Sponsor involvement is disclosed to students, and the UMass Chan OEE track follows UMass Chan conflict-of-interest and disclosure policies. The details are in the questions below.

For sponsors and hosts

What support can look like

Funding is helpful. So is hosting a Boston event, sending a speaker, judging a pitch night, or mentoring a student project.

What does sponsorship pay for?
The practical costs of running high-quality events: dinners, meeting rooms, Boston showcase logistics, speaker travel, and materials for project teams.
What does a sponsor get?
Acknowledgment on this page and, when appropriate, the option to send a speaker or mentor who fits the incubator. Sponsors do not receive student contact lists or student data.
Can a sponsor promote a product?
No. There is a firm no-product-promotion rule for any sponsor-supported session. Guests speak to their experience and the field, not to a product line.
How are conflicts of interest handled?
Everything follows UMass Chan conflict-of-interest and disclosure policies, and any sponsorship behind a session is disclosed to students up front.
Who manages the funds?
Funds will be stewarded through the student organization and program team, and spent only on the practical costs of running sessions, showcases, and project events.
Can we send a speaker instead of funding?
Yes. Sending a guest is welcome on its own. Speakers are chosen for fit with a session and follow the same guardrails as everyone else on the program.
