What you take away

By the end, you can

  • Describe the main ways physicians engage in innovation, from founder to advisor to investor.
  • Read a venture with a business model canvas: value, evidence, regulatory path, and how it gets paid for.
  • Name the regional players, the schools, incubators, accelerators, and programs like Nucleate.
  • Pull real lessons from startups that worked and startups that did not.
  • Hold your own in a room of founders and investors, and leave with people to follow up with.

Logistics

Format. Hybrid, mostly in person and dinner-style, with the occasional virtual guest. Where. Albert Sherman Center, UMass Chan, Worcester; the December session is off-site in Boston or Cambridge. Cap. 25 students, to keep it a conversation.

To complete it. Attend five of the six evenings, keep a short reflection after each, and build one one-page business model canvas. That earns a certificate of completion in Medical Entrepreneurship and Innovation.

Beyond the six evenings

Formats we are exploring

Ideas the team is weighing for this and future cohorts. Not all will run in the first year; they sketch where the elective can grow.

Founder dinners
Small dinners with a physician-founder telling one company's story start to finish, including what went wrong.
Pharma and biotech talks
An evening with someone from drug discovery or biotech leadership on how a therapeutic actually gets made.
Medtech career panel
Physicians and engineers on the different ways to work in and around medical devices.
UMass Lowell engineering collaboration
A joint session with engineering students on turning a clinical need into a device prototype.
Boston medical-schools meet-up
A shared evening with healthcare clubs at Harvard, BU, Tufts, and Harvard Business School.
Pitch and case competition
Student teams work a real case or pitch an early idea to a friendly panel for feedback.
Failed-startups night
A candid look at ventures that did not make it, and the lessons that outlast them.
Device incubator visit
An off-site visit to a medical-device incubator or accelerator to see early companies at work.
Consulting case study
Working a healthcare business or reimbursement problem the way a strategy team would.
Fundraiser and speaker panel
An evening that pairs a guest panel with light fundraising for the student organization.
Program-evaluation study
A short pre- and post-term survey of how students' understanding of healthcare innovation shifts, so the elective can improve and, in time, be written up.
