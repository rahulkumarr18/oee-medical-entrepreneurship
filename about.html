About

Bring an early medical idea. Pitch it, get feedback, and meet the people who can help build it.

We connect students, clinicians, engineers, professors, founders, and investors from across Massachusetts, plugged into the Boston corridor around Mass General and Harvard Medical School, and hospitals and universities across Massachusetts.

Why it exists

Clinical problems are often noticed long before anyone knows what kind of solution they need. A student sees a bottleneck. A resident watches a workaround become routine. A faculty member has a discovery that might leave the lab. An engineer wants a problem worth building for.

The hard part is knowing who should be in the next conversation. This incubator is built to make that easier: bring the problem into a room with people who understand science, care delivery, design, business models, reimbursement, policy, regulation, and funding.

What it is

You can do four things here: learn how medical ideas become real products, hear from founders and physicians who have built them, bring your own idea to pitch for feedback, and meet people who can help. Some come to teach, mentor, sponsor, or recruit.

For UMass Chan medical students, it can be taken for course credit as the OEE track (an Optional Enrichment Elective): sessions, short reflections, a one-page business plan, and a certificate. Anyone across Massachusetts is welcome at the public events, to meet people, pitch an early idea, and help it move forward.

Who it serves

Not only one school, and not only founders.

Medical students and traineesLearn how ideas move from bedside observations to evidence, prototypes, companies, and policy.
Professors and physiciansGive talks, mentor projects, and help students understand what the field looks like from the inside.
Engineers and buildersMeet clinicians with problems worth solving and learn the medical constraints around the work.
Founders and investorsFind emerging talent, give candid feedback, and help shape stronger healthcare ideas.

Where it happens

Worcester is the anchor. Boston is the stage.

The first cycle is based at UMass Chan in Worcester, then moves out to Boston and Cambridge, the heart of the country's medical-research corridor around Mass General and Harvard Medical School, for meetups, speaker dinners, and pitch nights. The goal is to make it easy for students from many schools and professionals across the region to take part.

Over time, the goal is to grow from an event series into a lasting Massachusetts program that helps early medical ideas find co-founders, mentors, funding, and a first real chance to prove they work.
