About

The part that comes after the observation

Medical school teaches you to find the problem. It rarely shows you what happens next.

The gap

Physicians sit closer to unmet needs than almost anyone. A workflow that wastes an hour, a device that does not fit the anatomy, a diagnostic that comes back too late. The core curriculum is built, correctly, around the science and the clinical skills every graduate needs. It does not set aside time for what happens after you notice the problem.

That next step has its own vocabulary and its own people: how an observation becomes a device, a diagnostic, a therapeutic, a company, or a change in policy; the difference between a founder, a scientific advisor, a clinical investigator, and an investor; how incubators, accelerators, and tech-transfer offices actually work; and where the money comes from. This elective covers that step, in plain terms, from people who have lived it.

Who it is for

You do not need a startup, a patent, or a plan. The elective is built for curious students first, and for would-be founders second. If you have ever wondered how the tools you use every day came to exist, that is enough of a reason to come.

It is meant to complement existing UMass Chan offerings such as EBID and Vista rather than duplicate them. Where those focus on their own aims, this one is deliberately about exposure and access: time with founders and investors, a working grasp of the regional ecosystem, and a low-stakes way to test whether this world is for you.

How it runs

Six evenings across the fall, every other Wednesday, over dinner on the UMass Chan campus in Worcester. Thirty minutes with a guest, thirty minutes of questions, thirty minutes of the actual point: talking to each other and to the speaker. The final session moves to Boston and Cambridge for a networking night.

Along the way you keep a short reflection after each session and build one simple business model canvas. Do that, show up for five of the six evenings, and you finish with a certificate of completion. See the program for the full schedule.

Get involved

Curious is enough

Come to one evening and see. The Fall 2026 cohort is capped at 25, so it stays a conversation rather than a lecture.
