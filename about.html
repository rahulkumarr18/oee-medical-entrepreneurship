About

A room where early medical ideas can become real conversations.

The incubator connects Massachusetts students, clinicians, researchers, engineers, founders, professors, and investors around practical healthcare entrepreneurship.

Why it exists

Clinical problems are often noticed long before anyone knows what kind of solution they need. A student sees a bottleneck. A resident watches a workaround become routine. A faculty member has a discovery that might leave the lab. An engineer wants a problem worth building for.

The hard part is knowing who should be in the next conversation. This incubator is built to make that easier: bring the problem into a room with people who understand science, care delivery, design, business models, reimbursement, policy, regulation, and funding.

What it is

It is part educational series, part speaker network, part pitch forum, and part early incubator. Some people will come to learn the medical technology landscape. Some will bring an idea. Some will come to teach, mentor, sponsor, or recruit promising teams.

For UMass Chan medical students, the incubator includes a credit-bearing OEE track with attendance, reflections, a business model canvas, and a certificate. For the wider Massachusetts community, the public-facing events are a way to meet people, present projects, and help ideas move forward.

Who it serves

Not only one school, and not only founders.

Medical students and traineesLearn how ideas move from bedside observations to evidence, prototypes, companies, and policy.
Professors and physiciansGive talks, mentor projects, and help students understand what the field looks like from the inside.
Engineers and buildersMeet clinicians with problems worth solving and learn the medical constraints around the work.
Founders and investorsFind emerging talent, give candid feedback, and help shape stronger healthcare ideas.

Where it happens

Worcester is the anchor. Boston is the stage.

The first cycle uses UMass Chan in Worcester as an academic home base, then moves outward for Boston and Cambridge meetups, speaker salons, and showcase nights. The goal is to make it easy for students from multiple schools and professionals across the region to participate.

Over time, the ambition is to grow from an event series into a durable Massachusetts medical entrepreneurship accelerator: a place where early clinical ideas can find teams, mentors, capital, and a first real test.
